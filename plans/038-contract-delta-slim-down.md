## Why this matters

The ghost contract computes and returns three result fields that nothing
reads: `nativeDelta`, `deltaTokens`, and `tokenDeltas`. The TypeScript decode
(`probeData` in `src/internal/simulator.ts`) consumes only `observedTokens`,
`maxTokenOutflows`, `maxNativeOutflow`, and the two checkpoint arrays plus
`balanceProbeOk`; the three delta fields are decoded and discarded. Computing
them costs a second `balanceOf` pass over every candidate at the end of the
simulation (`_writeTokenResults`) and inflates the returndata of every single
simulation. The ABI/bytecode are not public exports, so no supported external
consumer can observe the removal.

Per-call balance attribution (`BalanceDelta.byCall`, plan 034) is derived from
`balanceCheckpoints`, NOT from these fields — so removing them does not touch
the attribution path.

## Target design

`SimulationResult` keeps exactly: `success`, `failingCallIndex`, `revertData`,
`observedTokens`, `maxTokenOutflows`, `maxNativeOutflow`, `allowanceCheckpoints`,
`balanceCheckpoints`, `balanceProbeOk`. The three delta fields are gone.
`maxTokenOutflows` is still computed — but from the min-balance sweep already
tracked in `TokenState`, so it moves out of `_writeTokenResults` into
`simulate()` directly (or a slimmed helper). `_writeTokenResults`, `_signedDelta`,
`_trimInts`, and `MAX_INT256` are deleted. The second end-of-simulation
`balanceOf` sweep disappears with them.

The ABI string in `simulator.ts` and the ABI mirror in `fakeClient.ts` drop the
three fields in the same commit. Bytecode is regenerated via
`pnpm build:contracts`.

## Steps

### Step 1: Slim the contract

In `contracts/TxSimulator.sol`:
1. Remove `int256 nativeDelta`, `address[] deltaTokens`, `int256[] tokenDeltas`
   from `SimulationResult`.
2. In `simulate()`, delete the `result.nativeDelta = _signedDelta(...)` line and
   the `_writeTokenResults(...)` call. Fill `result.maxTokenOutflows` from the
   `TokenState` min-balance data inline (or via a slimmed helper) — keep
   `observedTokens`/`maxNativeOutflow` untouched.
3. Delete `_writeTokenResults`, `_signedDelta`, `_trimInts`, and the now-unused
   `MAX_INT256` constant. Keep `_trimAddresses` (still used for `observedTokens`).

**Verify**: `grep -n "_writeTokenResults\|_signedDelta\|_trimInts\|MAX_INT256\|nativeDelta\|deltaTokens\|tokenDeltas" contracts/TxSimulator.sol` → no matches.

### Step 2: Update the ABI strings

Drop `int256 nativeDelta;`, `address[] deltaTokens;`, `int256[] tokenDeltas;`
